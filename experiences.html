One day on the island.

06:40

Nagoa, before anyone

The bay is glass at first light. Swim out over the shallows, or walk the shell line while the palms are still grey.

Bicycles wait by the courtyard gate — the ride west is flat and empty.

Dawn photo
09:00

Vanakbara harbour

Painted trawlers three-deep, gulls arguing over the catch, nets drying on the sea wall at the island's fishing end.

The boats unload early — ask the desk to pack chá for the drive.

Harbour photo
11:30

The fortress walls

Diu Fort's ramparts run high above the surf — cannon, coral stone, and the lighthouse at the island's eastern point.

The moat path stays shaded until noon. Go clockwise.

Fort photo
13:30

A long lunch

Grilled catch, lime soda, ceiling fans. Lunch in Diu is not a pause in the day — it is the day, for a while.

Order whatever came off the Vanakbara boats this morning.

Lunch photo
16:30

The Portuguese quarter

St. Paul's baroque façade, painted houses leaning together, and chá in a shaded courtyard as the streets wake back up.

The church is quietest before evening mass.

Old town photo
19:12

Sunset from the ramparts

The sea goes brass, the lighthouse takes over, and dinner is waiting back at the courtyard.

Sunset moves through the year — the desk keeps today's time.
